About

I am currently a research associate working with Brad Love at the University College London. In the past, I worked with Mark Wheeler at the Center for Advanced Neuroimaging at the Georgia Institute of Technology, and with Carol Seger at Colorado State University, where I completed my Ph.D. Broadly, I combine neuroimaging (primarily fMRI), behavioral experimentation, mathematical modeling, and modern “machine-learning” approaches to investigate biological mechanisms underlying our ability to draw meaningful information from the world around us. My work is closely related to the fields of categorization and decision-making.
